Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come in your mind related to IPO.

  • Step 1: Login to your respective service provider.
  • Step 2: Click on the IPO button.
  • Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid and enter the relevant details.
  • Step 4: Your subscription will be completed once you make the payment or give permission.

It's generally recommended to wait for the IPO to list and stabilize before making a purchase decision. This allows you to assess the company's performance and market sentiment before investing.

To determine if an IPO is good, consider the following factors:
  • Company's financial performance and growth prospects
  • Industry trends and market demand
  • Management team's experience and track record
  • Valuation and pricing of the IPO
  • Risk factors and potential challenges

You can check the IPO start date on the company's website, stock exchange website, or through your broker's platform.

The issue size refers to the total amount of money that a company aims to raise through an IPO. It is typically expressed in terms of the number of shares being offered and the price range for each share.

The number of shares in a lot varies depending on the IPO and the company. It is usually specified in the IPO prospectus or offering document.

The lot size is typically calculated based on the minimum investment amount required for the IPO. It may also depend on the company's capital structure and the number of shares being offered.

The IPO price band is typically decided by the company in consultation with its investment bankers and underwriters.

IPO GMP stands for Grey Market Premium, which is the premium at which IPO shares are traded in the grey market before they are listed on the stock exchange.

The number of lots you should apply for an IPO depends on your investment goals, risk tolerance, and financial situation. It's recommended to consult with a financial advisor or broker to determine the appropriate number of lots for your individual circumstances.
